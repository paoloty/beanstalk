History of Roasted Soybeans as
Coffee Substitue

In 1934 Henry Ford served "Roasted Soybean Coffee" to the press at a gala soyfoods luncheon at the Chicago World's
Fair. By 1936 J.H. Kellogg's Battle Creek Food Company was marketing Soy Kee, a "100% soybean roasted coffee
substitute," and Madison Foods, had introduced Soy-Koff (containing roasted soybeans, bran, and brown sugar) in
regular and fine grinds. Also in 1936 a soy coffee brand-named Soyco, and in 1938 Radcliff's 100% Soya Bean Beverage
(coffee substitute) were being sold in Los Angeles health food stores. The first American recipe for homemade soy
coffee appeared in Jethro Kloss' Back to Eden (1939).
